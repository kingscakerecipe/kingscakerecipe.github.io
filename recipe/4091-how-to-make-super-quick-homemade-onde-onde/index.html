The ingredients needed to make Onde Onde:
  1. Prepare 1/2 Cup Desiccated Coconut,
  2. You need Pinch Sea Salt,
  3. Get 75 g Palm Sugar Coarsely Chopped,
  4. Take Pandan Leaves Coarsely Chopped, 10
  5. Prepare 150 ml Water,
  6. Provide 125 g Glutinous Rice Flour,
  7. Take 1/2 TSP Pure Pandan Paste,
Instructions to make Onde Onde:
  1. In a large heat proof shallow bowl, add desiccated coconut and salt. - - Stir to combine well. - - Prepare a steamer and steam the coconut mixture for about 5 to 10 mins or until the coconut has softened. - - Remove from heat and set aside.
  2. Prepare the filling. - - In a spice grinder, add palm sugar. - - Blitz until fine. - - Using a 1/3 tsp, scoop and lay each individual scoops onto a baking tray lined with parchment paper. - - You should have 15 scoops. - - Cover with cling film and sit in the freezer until frozen.
  3. Prepare the dough. - - In a blender, add pandan leaves and water. - - Blitz until smooth. - - Drain the pandan juice into a bowl thru' a strainer. - - Using the back of a spoon, squeeze out any excess juice.
  4. Discard the residue. - - You should have 1/2 cup of pandan juice. - - If you have less, add water until it reaches 1/2 cup. Discard any excess. - - Prepare a sauce pot with about 1/2 full with water over medium-high heat.
  5. Bring to a gentle boil while preparing the dough. - - In a large bowl, add in glutinous rice flour, pandan juice and pandan paste. - - Mix to combine well. - - Mix until it comes together into a dough.
  6. The dough should be tacky and smooth. - - Pinch 20g of the dough and drop into the pot of boiling water. - - As soon as the dough float to the surface, scoop out using a cooking spider.
  7. Do not off the heat, turn the heat down to low and let it simmer.* - - Transfer the blanched dough into the rest of the dough. - - Continue to knead until well combined. - - The dough should be a tacky and smooth, but not sticky. - - *If the dough is too dry, add in 1 tsp of water. if the dough is too sticky, add in 1 tsp of flour.*
  8. Assemble the onde onde. - - Bring the pot of water up to a gentle boil by turning the heat up to medium. - - Pinch 15g of the dough. - - Cover the rest of the dough with damp lint free kitchen towel. This will prevent the rest of the dough from drying out.
  9. Flatten the dough into a disc. - - Take 1 of the fillings from the freezer. - - Leave the rest of the fillings in the freezer or they will melt super fast at room temperature.
  10. Put the filling in the middle of the flatten dough. - - Seal the filling with your fingers. - - Make sure it is sealed properly. - - Shape it into a round ball.
  11. Drop the dough ball into the boiling water. - - Stir the water to prevent the dough ball from sticking at the bottom of the pot. - - Once the dough float to the top, allow it to boil for another couple of mins. - - Scoop the dough ball out with a cooking spider.
  12. Transfer the dough ball onto the steamed desiccated coconut. - - Roll the dough until fully coated. - - Repeat the process for the remaining onde onde. - - Do not shape and fill the onde onde all at once, the filling will melt and the onde onde will dry out. Work on 1 dough at a time. - - Serve immediately.
